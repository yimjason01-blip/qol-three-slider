Quality of Life · Calibration Spec v1

Operationalizes the three-axis QoL teaching tool into a measurable, fittable model. Names every instrument, declares every weight, sequences the data work. Not a clinical instrument until calibrated against the cohorts named below.

Purpose

IN BRIEF This spec converts the three-axis prototype from a teaching tool into a calibrated model. Each axis becomes a measurable composite of named instruments with declared weights and a normative reference distribution. The QoL curve becomes a longitudinal fit against UK Biobank with HRS validation. The line render becomes a regression of felt vitality against reserve and lifestyle, with an age-decay term fit from longitudinal trajectories. Avg American becomes a NHANES + BRFSS pull rather than an eyeballed anchor.

The prototype's architecture (three orthogonal axes, geometry vs render split, age-fading vitality) is held fixed. The calibration changes what's behind the labels, not what the user sees. The end-user surface is essentially unchanged after calibration; the rigor moves from prose disclaimers ("illustrative · not calibrated") into the methodology this spec defines.

Defined Terms

This document uses the following terms with specific meanings. They are referenced throughout the body without re-definition.

Risk · residualized
10-year probability of a major adverse cardiovascular event, residualized against the reserve composite. The portion of risk not already explained by capacity.
Reserve · capacity
Multi-domain functional capacity composite: cardiorespiratory, musculoskeletal, skeletal, cognitive, balance, pulmonary. Age- and sex-normalized to a 0–100 percentile.
Lifestyle · felt
Composite of validated patient-reported outcomes covering felt vitality dimensions that are non-physical and non-capacity. Sleep, mood, stress, connection, purpose, drive, libido, pain, fatigue.
Vitality (felt)
The single-construct outcome that the line render encodes. v1 uses SF-36 Vitality (4 items, 0–100). Distinct from the Lifestyle composite, which is one of the two predictors of Vitality.
Quality of Life
The curve's y-axis. Equal-weighted composite of physical function, cognitive function, social function, and independence at age A. Bounded 0–100. Death = 0.
Render mix
The 0–1 quantity that drives line color, width, and glow. Currently 0.5·Reserve + 0.5·Lifestyle. Calibrated form: regression coefficients from the Vitality fit.
Age decay
The age-driven attenuation of the render mix. Currently a logistic with floor, slope, and center modulated by reserve. Calibrated form: parameters fit from longitudinal vitality trajectories.
Avg American
The dashed reference curve and preset. The median U.S. adult's position on each axis, computed from public-health surveillance data.

1 · Risk · residualized

IN BRIEF Risk is operationalized as 10-year MACE probability from the AHA PREVENT model, residualized against the reserve composite to remove the variance reserve already explains. The slider value is the residual ranked against the NHANES adult distribution. v1 is cardiovascular only; cancer, dementia, and fracture are deferred to v2. Adults 30–79 to stay in PREVENT validity range.

Construct

10-year probability of MI, stroke, cardiovascular death, or hospitalized heart failure.

Base model

PREVENT (Predicting Risk of CVD EVENTs), AHA 2024. Inputs: age, sex, total cholesterol, HDL-C, SBP, antihypertensive use, current smoking, diabetes, eGFR, BMI, statin use. Optional: UACR, A1c, social deprivation index.

Residualization procedure

1. PREVENT_pct = percentile(PREVENT 10yr CVD, NHANES adults) 2. predicted = β₀ + β₁ · Reserve_pct [linear regression on NHANES] 3. residual = PREVENT_pct − predicted 4. Risk_slider = percentile(residual, residual_distribution)

Slider mapping

  • 0 = lowest 1st percentile residualized risk after accounting for reserve
  • 100 = highest 99th percentile residualized risk
  • Avg American = computed from NHANES (replaces eyeballed 65)

v1 simplifications

  • Linear residualization. v2 may use GAM or quantile regression if nonlinearity is meaningful.
  • Single 10-year time horizon.
  • Cardiovascular events only; multi-domain risk deferred.
  • Adults 30–79; pediatric and very-elderly out of scope.

2 · Reserve · capacity

IN BRIEF Reserve is an eight-component composite covering the major domains of functional capacity. Each component is normalized to age- and sex-adjusted percentile against NHANES (US) or HUNT4 (Norway, more complete activity data), then weighted and summed to a 0–100 score. v1 weights are clinical-prior; v2 derives them by regressing all-cause mortality against the components in UK Biobank. Avg American is computed by composing each NHANES component median.

Normalization

For each component i: z_i = (raw_i − μ(age, sex)) / σ(age, sex) [reference: NHANES or HUNT4] p_i = Φ(z_i) · 100 Reserve_pct = Σ wᵢ · pᵢ

Slider mapping

  • 0 = severe deconditioning, composite below 5th percentile
  • 50 = population median for age and sex
  • 100 = top 1% for age and sex
  • Avg American = composed from NHANES component medians (replaces eyeballed 28)

v2 weight derivation

Fit a Cox proportional-hazards model for 10-year all-cause mortality on the eight components in UK Biobank. Take the standardized hazard-ratio coefficients, normalize their absolute values to sum to 1.0, use as weights. This replaces clinical-prior weights with mortality-anchored weights.

3 · Lifestyle · felt

IN BRIEF Lifestyle is a weighted composite of validated patient-reported outcomes covering ten non-physical, non-capacity dimensions. Each instrument is converted to a 0–100 percentile against its published reference distribution and weighted to a single score. The avg-American anchor combines BRFSS sleep and mental-health prevalences with NHANES Mental Health module and ELSA-equivalent loneliness data.

Normalization

PROMIS instruments use T-scores (population mean 50, SD 10) which convert directly to percentile via Φ. PSQI, PHQ-9, GAD-7, UCLA-3, BPI, MLQ, FSFI/IIEF each have published reference distributions (general population norms). For each, convert raw → reference percentile (0–100), reverse-coded where higher raw = worse (PSQI, PHQ-9, GAD-7, UCLA-3, BPI). Weighted sum.

Slider mapping

  • 0 = chronic poor, composite below 5th percentile
  • 50 = population median
  • 100 = optimal felt vitality, top 1%
  • Avg American = computed from BRFSS + NHANES + national PRO surveillance (replaces eyeballed 42)

4 · Quality of Life curve

IN BRIEF The QoL outcome variable is an equal-weighted composite of physical function, cognitive function, social function, and independence, scored 0–100 with death = 0. The curve geometry is a logistic with floor, ceiling, midpoint, and slope as linear functions of risk and reserve. Coefficients are fit from UK Biobank longitudinal data stratified by risk-quartile × reserve-quartile, with HRS validation. Sex-stratified in v1.

Outcome variable definition

DomainInstrumentWeight
Physical functionPROMIS Physical Function 8b → 0–10025%
Cognitive functionMoCA → 0–10025%
Social functionPROMIS Ability to Participate25%
IndependenceLawton IADL + Katz ADL composite25%

Functional form

QoL(age) = floor + (ceil − floor) / (1 + exp(slope · (age − midpoint))) floor = β₀ + β₁ · Reserve + β₂ · Risk ceil = β₃ + β₄ · Reserve + β₅ · Risk midpoint = β₆ + β₇ · Reserve + β₈ · Risk slope = β₉ + β₁₀ · Reserve + β₁₁ · Risk

Fit procedure

  1. Stratify cohort into 16 groups: Reserve quartile × Risk quartile.
  2. Within each group, fit a logistic curve to mean QoL vs age across waves.
  3. Regress the four logistic parameters (floor, ceil, midpoint, slope) against the underlying continuous Reserve and Risk values to recover β₀…β₁₁.
  4. Repeat sex-stratified.
  5. Validate against HRS held-out cohort.

Cohort

Primary: UK Biobank (~500k, repeat assessments, comprehensive biomarkers and capacity tests). Validation: HRS (US, ages 50+, biennial waves). Tertiary: ELSA (UK, ages 50+).

5 · Line render

IN BRIEF The render encodes felt vitality, defined as SF-36 Vitality scale (0–100). Base render is a regression of vitality against reserve and lifestyle. Age decay is fit from longitudinal vitality trajectories stratified by reserve, modulating the render mix to produce visible fade across the lifespan. Both fits use UK Biobank cross-sectional and HRS longitudinal data.

Vitality construct

SF-36 Vitality (4 items: feel full of life, have energy, feel worn out, feel tired). Scored 0–100, higher = better. Selected over PROMIS Global because of cleaner single-construct definition and longer longitudinal data history.

Base regression

Vitality = α + β_R · Reserve_pct + β_L · Lifestyle_pct + ε Render mix (calibrated) = (β_R · Reserve + β_L · Lifestyle) / (β_R · 100 + β_L · 100)

Replaces current eyeballed 0.5/0.5 weights. Empirical coefficients may be unequal — likely β_L > β_R because lifestyle directly samples felt-state instruments while reserve samples capacity.

Age decay regression

Vitality(age, Reserve) = floor(R) + (1 − floor(R)) / (1 + exp(k(R) · (age − center(R)))) floor(R) = γ₀ + γ₁ · Reserve_pct k(R) = γ₂ + γ₃ · Reserve_pct center(R) = γ₄ + γ₅ · Reserve_pct

Fit by stratifying age trajectories of vitality by reserve quartile in HRS (longitudinal vitality data ages 50–95+). Recover γ₀…γ₅ from logistic fits per stratum, then linear fit against continuous reserve.

6 · Avg American anchor

IN BRIEF Replaces three eyeballed numbers (Risk 65, Reserve 28, Lifestyle 42) with composite scores computed from public-health surveillance data. NHANES is the primary source; BRFSS and the Surgeon General's loneliness reports fill gaps. Each composite is built using the same instrument list and weights defined in sections 1–3.

8 · Open questions and decisions still required

  1. Single cohort vs harmonized. UK Biobank has the depth but is a healthy-volunteer-biased UK adult cohort. Adding HRS and ELSA improves generalization but adds harmonization work.
  2. Stratification beyond sex. v1 is sex-stratified, race-blind. Race/ethnicity stratification is a near-term decision with both validity and ethical implications.
  3. Multi-domain risk vs CVD-only. v1 is CVD via PREVENT. Cancer (LCBC, BCSC), dementia (CAIDE, LIBRA), and fall/fracture (FRAX) all have validated 10-year models. Combining them into one all-event probability vs carrying separately is a real product decision.
  4. DEXA reliance. Reserve leans on DEXA-derived ALMI and bone density; DEXA isn't routine. Substituting BIA and screening-questionnaire bone risk loses precision.
  5. Vitality construct. SF-36 Vitality is older and well-validated; PROMIS Global is newer and modular. v1 picks SF-36 for longitudinal data depth.
  6. Update cadence. Once calibrated, weights and reference distributions decay. Annual re-fit against latest NHANES is the floor; quarterly is overkill.
  7. Personalization vs universal. This spec defines the universal teaching tool. The personalized clinical instrument requires different surface decisions (today marker, KPIs, action library) but the calibration carries over.

What stays valid before calibration

The structural design choices survive recalibration: three orthogonal axes, geometry vs render split, age-fading vitality, residualization to keep risk and reserve independent, no input appearing in two columns. Those are model architecture. The numbers, weights, and curve parameters are what calibration replaces. The end-user surface remains essentially identical.
