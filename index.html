Functional Capacity Across the Lifespan

A teaching tool for internalizing patterns. The y-axis is VO₂max, the best single measure of physical capability across age. Three levers shape the curve: Conditioning (the dominant lever — protects against events too), Lifestyle Optimization (drives line color, small slope and event effect), and Inherited Risk (genetic and structural propensity for events). All three levers combine to determine when events render. Not a personalized prediction. Decision logs.

Two events fork: cancer treatment (~age 65) and CV event (~age 75). Toggle on to see how probability splits the trajectory.

Three things this tool simplifies
  • Cardiovascular events drop conditioning in reality. The Conditioning slider stays where you set it; the event step-down is shown on the curve, but the slider position does not move when an event hits.
  • Lifestyle inputs feed conditioning over years. Sleep, stress, hormone optimization affect training capacity and therefore conditioning. The tool treats them as orthogonal at the rendering moment — what you set is what renders.
  • Inherited Risk is a single dial, but real risk profiles are domain-specific. A person can have high cardiovascular risk and low cancer risk. The slider here represents aggregate propensity for the events shown.
