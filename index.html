Functional Capacity Across the Lifespan

A teaching tool for internalizing patterns. The y-axis is VO₂max, the best single measure of physical capability across age. Three levers shape the curve: Conditioning (the dominant one), Lifestyle Optimization (mostly visible as line color), and Residual Risk (introduces events). Not a personalized prediction. Five things this tool simplifies
  • Conditioning lowers cardiovascular and metabolic event risk in reality. The tool teaches Conditioning and Residual Risk as separate levers and does not bake the protective effect into event probability.
  • Lifestyle Optimization (sleep, nutrition, stress) affects cardiovascular and metabolic event risk in reality. The tool does not model this; the levers are taught separately.
  • Cardiovascular events drop conditioning in reality. The Conditioning slider stays where you set it; the event step-down is shown on the curve, but the slider position does not move.
  • Lifestyle inputs feed conditioning over years. Sleep, stress, hormone optimization affect training capacity and therefore conditioning. The tool treats them as orthogonal at the rendering moment.
  • Lifestyle Optimization affects whether metabolic syndrome and other risk states develop over time. The tool models the lever (behavior) and the state (Residual Risk) separately. Move Lifestyle Optimization to set behavior; move Residual Risk to set the state.
