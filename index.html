What the curve teaches. Risk lowers the late-life floor by raising event probability. Reserve raises the floor and shifts the inflection point later, so decline starts later and is gentler. Together they produce the shape — the geometry that determines how the back half of life goes.

What the line teaches. Reserve and lifestyle together render brightness, color, and weight. High reserve alone produces a respectable glow because reserve genuinely contributes to felt vitality. Lifestyle adds the rest. The line carries the ride — how alive the system feels as it travels the curve.

What you can read off the model. A bright line on a collapsing curve is the person who feels great today on a trajectory that is quietly failing. A muted line on a beautiful curve is the person whose biology is intact but who is not enjoying the journey. Both are real, and the model has to be able to show both honestly. That is the test the three axes pass — and the test the original two-axis (risk + reserve = quality of life) framing fails.
